Is It Love… or Love Bombing?

🚩 Red Alert: When the “I love you” drops before your Starbucks order does, it’s time to pause.

Let’s be real — love bombing is like getting emotional whiplash. It’s intense, overwhelming, and low-key addictive. At first, it feels like a rom-com montage: constant compliments, rapid-fire texting, big promises (“You’re the one,” “I’ve never felt like this”). But beneath that fairytale haze is something much darker.

What is Love Bombing?

Psychologists define love bombing as “an attempt to influence a person by demonstrations of attention and affection” (Psychology Today). It’s a classic tactic used by narcissists and emotional abusers to gain control quickly.

“Love bombing is not love. It’s manipulation disguised as affection.” – Dr. Ramani Durvasula

Real Example

Their message:
“You’re not like anyone I’ve ever met. I’ve been looking for you my whole life. I want to wake up next to you forever.” (after 5 days of texting)

Decoded:
Intense emotional declarations with zero context = 🚩. This isn’t soulmates, it’s strategy. Emotional intimacy without real-world connection is a manipulation technique — not fate.

Why It Works

  • 🧠 Our brains release dopamine + oxytocin during high attention = instant bond
  • ⏱️ It’s designed to move fast — so you don’t have time to think critically
  • 😢 After the high comes the withdrawal — often used to create dependency

Green Flag Check-In ✅

  • Do they respect your pace?
  • Can you say “not yet” without guilt?
  • Do their actions match their words?
