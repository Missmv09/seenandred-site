Trust Your Intuition

Patterns & Psychology

Published: July 10, 2024 • ~7 min read

Your gut can be wise—and it can also be wired by past pain. Use this 4‑part check to decide which voice you’re hearing.

Intuition vs. Anxiety

  • Intuition: brief, clear, calm directive.
  • Anxiety: noisy, catastrophic, repetitive.

4‑Part Gut Check

  1. Body: Breathe, then scan—tight chest = slow down.
  2. Data: What behaviours have you actually seen?
  3. Dialogue: Ask one direct question; listen for consistency.
  4. Decision: Make a small boundary, not a grand gesture.

Practice Reps

  • Say: “I like consistency. Can we pick a time that works weekly?”
  • Pause texting when spiraling; move your body first.
  • Journal one green flag per date; train your attention.

FAQ: Trusting Your Intuition

What if my gut is always anxious?

Regulate first (sleep, food, movement), then decide. Intuition is calm and brief; anxiety is loud and repetitive.

Is one “bad” text enough to end it?

One text is data, not destiny. Patterns decide. Track 2–3 similar moments before making a call.

How do I ask a clarifying question without sounding needy?

Try: “I value consistency. Can we pick a weekly time that works for both of us?”
